Who is Bartholomäus Ziegenbalg?

Bartholomäus Ziegenbalg (1682–1719) was a German Lutheran missionary who is renowned for being the first Protestant missionary to India. He embarked on a mission to the Danish colony of Tranquebar (modern-day Tharangambadi) in South India in 1706. Ziegenbalg's pioneering efforts included translating the Bible and hymns into Tamil, establishing schools, and promoting Christian education and healthcare. His work laid the foundation for Protestantism in India and left a lasting impact on Christian missions and education in the region.

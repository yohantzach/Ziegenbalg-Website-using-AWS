Ziegenbalg's Journey and Achievements

  • 1683 - Birth

    Bartholomäus Ziegenbalg is born on July 10 in Pulsnitz, Electorate of Saxony, Holy Roman Empire (now Germany).

  • 1705 - Journey to India

    Ziegenbalg arrives in Tranquebar, India, as the first Protestant missionary from Europe to India.

  • 1706 - Establishment of Mission

    Ziegenbalg establishes the first Protestant mission in India and begins his work of translating the Bible into Tamil.

  • 1709 - Completion of Tamil Bible

    Ziegenbalg completes the translation of the entire Bible into Tamil, making it the first complete Protestant Bible translation in an Indian language.

  • 1711 - Founding of a Seminary

    Ziegenbalg establishes a seminary in Tranquebar to train native pastors and teachers.

  • 1714 - Tamil Grammar

    Ziegenbalg publishes the first Tamil grammar book, enabling further linguistic and educational advancements.

  • 1715 - Return to Europe

    Ziegenbalg returns to Europe due to health issues but continues to advocate for the mission in India.

  • 1719 - Death

    Bartholomäus Ziegenbalg passes away in Tranquebar on February 23, leaving behind a significant legacy in the history of Indian Christianity.
